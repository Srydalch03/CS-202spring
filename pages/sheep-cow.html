Steers

Above are 4 different photos of a steers. When it comes to judging all types of market livestock it is importent to rember the 5 structurs of judging. In order of most importent to least

  1. Muscle:This is the overall coverage of the muscle in comparison to fat on the steer
  2. Volume:This is how large is the animal, but don't think of it as how much meat am I going to get off when it is butchard
  3. Balance:Have you ever seen an animal and thought that their legs were to short for their body that is Balance
  4. Structure: Structure and Balance are interchangeable on anyday, the structure is what allows the animal to move, thisis often judged by how they stand or walk.
  5. Bone:The animal needs to be able to suport the muscle that is requestted. The Bone needs to be proportionet.

Sheep

When judging market lambs, evaluators look for animals that offer the most value in terms of meat production, structural soundness, and overall balance. Successful lambs should be well-muscled, correctly built, and show strong indicators of performance and finish.

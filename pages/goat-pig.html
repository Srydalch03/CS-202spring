Goat and Pig Standards

Pigs

I will be the first to admit that Pigs and goats are not my best classes, n/ but that is mainly because I am hardwired to look for such aspects that you see in cows and sheep. The important thing to rember for pigs is to look for the shape of a coke bottle. This shape should not be overly drastic but you should be able to easily Identify where the Ribs, Shoulders, and Butt start and end.

  • Muscle: Thick through the ham and loin. Look for width across the top and fullness in the center.
  • Finish:This Refers to the Fat amount on the pig. Should be adequate (not excessive) for market readiness
  • Structure & Movement:Correct feet and legs, strong pasterns, smooth stride.
  • Volume & CapacityDeep-sided, wide-chested, and good body length.
  • Balance Even proportions front to back with an alert, clean head.
  • Identification traits:Use traits like ear set (up or floppy), body markings, or tailplacement in oral reasons.

Goats

Goats are another species that I personally have diffulty Judging. Goats you would assume are just a smaller version of a sheep, However when it comes down to it you have to consider what tastes better. On a Goat the Fat is beneficial and tastes good. On the other hand sheep fat leaves a nasty after taste in the consumers mouth. For this reason Goats tend to be more on the fattier side. Looking at a small list to focus what you need to look for in a goat.

  • Muscle: Evaluate width and depth in the loin, leg, and rack. Look for shape anddefinition, especially over the top and down the leg.
  • Structure:Straight top lines, correct angles in shoulders and hocks, and solid feet and pasterns are critical for movement and longevity
  • Balance & Style:A clean-fronted, level-designed goat with a good length of body and symmetry between front and rear
  • Identification traits:Use traits like horn shape, coat color/pattern, and ear style in reasons.
